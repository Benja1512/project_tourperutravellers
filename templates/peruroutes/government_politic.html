Governance in Peru: An Overview of the Executive, Legislative, and Judicial Branche

The government of Peru is organized as a unitary presidential constitutional republic, with a clear separation of powers among the executive, legislative, and judicial branches.

1. The President of Peru serves as both head of state and head of government, elected for a five-year term with the possibility of non-consecutive terms. The Council of Ministers, composed of presidential appointees, oversees government departments and implements the president's policies​

2. Legislative Branch Congress of the Republic: Peru has a unicameral legislature composed of 130 members, who are elected for five-year terms. The Congress is responsible for creating laws, approving budgets, and exercising oversight over the executive branch. It has faced criticism for its fragmentation and high levels of corruption, with many members currently under investigation​.

3.- Judicial Branch Judiciary: The judicial system is independent and consists of various levels of courts, with the Supreme Court at the top. The judiciary faces challenges related to corruption and political interference, and reforms to strengthen judicial independence are ongoing.
