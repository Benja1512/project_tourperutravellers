Arequipa, The White City: A Must-Visit Destination in the Heart of Southern Peru Welcome to Arequipa, a hidden gem nestled among the majestic Peruvian Andes, known as "The White City" for its stunning colonial architecture built from sillar, a white volcanic stone. This city not only dazzles with its impressive buildings and landscapes but also offers a rich blend of history, culture, and nature, making it an unforgettable tourist destination. Located at the foot of the towering Misti volcano, Arequipa enchants visitors with its welcoming atmosphere and unparalleled cultural heritage. From the impressive Plaza de Armas, considered one of the most beautiful squares in South America, to the Santa Catalina Monastery, a fascinating religious complex that feels like a city within a city, every corner of Arequipa transports you back to colonial times. But Arequipa isn’t just about history. The city also serves as the gateway to stunning natural wonders such as the awe-inspiring Colca Canyon, one of the deepest in the world. Here, adventurers can enjoy unforgettable hiking trails and witness the majestic flight of the Andean condor. Arequipa’s gastronomy is another highlight. Known for its variety and bold flavors, it offers iconic dishes such as rocoto relleno, chupe de camarones, and the traditional ocopa. Visitors can indulge in these culinary delights at typical picanterías, where food becomes a true cultural experience. In Arequipa, the contrast between the old and the modern, the urban and the natural, invites you to experience something truly unique. Its year-round sunny climate, hospitality, and the warmth of its people make this city the perfect place to relax, explore, and fall in love with Peru. We invite you to discover Arequipa, a destination full of beauty, culture, and adventure that you simply can’t miss on your journey through Peru. 2. Mountain Biking The rugged terrain surrounding Arequipa provides excellent opportunities for mountain biking. The region offers a mix of thrilling downhill rides, scenic routes through the valleys, and technical off-road trails: Chachani Downhill: For experienced bikers, this thrilling descent from the Chachani Volcano offers incredible views and a fast-paced ride down rugged volcanic paths. Colca Valley: For a more scenic ride, biking through the Colca Valley allows you to explore traditional villages, terraced landscapes, and local wildlife at a slower pace.

3. Whitewater Rafting The Chili River, which runs through Arequipa, is a fantastic spot for whitewater rafting. Depending on the season, you can enjoy rapids ranging from Class II to Class IV, making it suitable for both beginners and more experienced rafters. The stretch of the river near the Chilina Valley offers beautiful canyon scenery, with steep cliffs and green valleys.

4. Rock Climbing Arequipa's volcanic rock formations provide excellent opportunities for rock climbing. The nearby Catarindo Climbing Area is a popular spot, offering routes for all skill levels, from beginners to advanced climbers. The volcanic sillar rock offers unique textures and holds, adding an exciting challenge to the climb.

5. Paragliding For those seeking an adrenaline rush combined with breathtaking aerial views, paragliding over the Arequipa countryside is a must. The takeoff spots near the Chili Valley and Yura allow you to soar over mountains, valleys, and rivers while enjoying panoramic views of the city and the surrounding volcanoes.

6. Horseback Riding Explore the beautiful countryside of Arequipa on horseback. Horseback tours often take you through the Sabandía and Yumina valleys, where you can admire traditional Andean farms, water mills, and picturesque landscapes. This is a more relaxed activity, perfect for families and those looking to connect with nature in a peaceful setting.

7. Sandboarding Just a short drive from Arequipa, you can experience sandboarding on the dunes of the nearby desert areas like the Acarí or Camaná. This activity is similar to snowboarding but on sand, providing a thrilling ride down the steep dunes for adventure seekers.

8. Birdwatching For those who enjoy nature and wildlife, birdwatching is a fantastic activity in the Colca Valley and Salinas y Aguada Blanca National Reserve. You’ll have the chance to see a variety of bird species, including the majestic Andean condor, one of the largest flying birds in the world, as well as flamingos, eagles, and Andean geese.

Cultural and Walking Tours For those who prefer a more relaxed pace, walking tours around Arequipa’s historic center are a great way to explore the city's colonial architecture, beautiful plazas, and museums.
