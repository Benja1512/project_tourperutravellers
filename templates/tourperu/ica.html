ICA Tour

Ica, a picturesque region in southern Peru, is renowned for its stunning desert landscapes, fertile valleys, and rich cultural heritage, offering visitors a glimpse into its ancient history through archaeological sites and vibrant wineries that produce the famous Pisco.

All the activities you can do on your trip to the Essence of Peru!

Lima Tour

    Historical Significance – Lima, known as the "City of Kings," has a rich colonial history. The Historic Center of Lima is a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring stunning architecture, including the Government Palace, Cathedral of Lima, and San Francisco Monastery, which showcases the city’s colonial past and the influence of Spanish culture.
    Culinary Scene – Lima is renowned as a gastronomic capital, offering a diverse culinary landscape. The city boasts several world-class restaurants that feature traditional Peruvian dishes, such as Ceviche and Aji de Gallina. A culinary tour can provide insight into local ingredients and cooking techniques, making it a must-do for food lovers.
    Cultural Experiences – Engaging with local culture is essential when visiting Lima. You can explore vibrant neighborhoods like Barranco, known for its bohemian vibe, colorful street art, and local galleries. Participating in cultural activities, such as dance performances or artisan workshops, offers a deeper understanding of Peru’s rich traditions and contemporary life.

Cuzco Tour

    Cultural Heritage – Cusco, the historic capital of the Inca Empire, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site rich in cultural heritage. The city features a blend of Inca and colonial architecture, with notable sites like the Qorikancha (Temple of the Sun) and the Cusco Cathedral. Exploring the vibrant streets and traditional markets, such as San Pedro Market, allows visitors to experience the local culture firsthand.
    Sacsayhuamán Fortress – Just outside the city, the Sacsayhuamán fortress is an impressive archaeological site known for its massive stone walls and incredible construction techniques. This Inca site provides stunning panoramic views of Cusco and offers insights into the engineering prowess of the Incas. The nearby Q'enqo, Puka Pukara, and Tambomachay ruins are also worth visiting, showcasing the significance of the area.
    Gateway to Machu Picchu – Cusco serves as the main gateway to Machu Picchu, one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. Many travelers begin their journey to the famous Inca citadel from Cusco, making it essential to explore the town’s rich history and prepare for the trek or train ride to this iconic site. The Sacred Valley, located nearby, offers breathtaking landscapes and additional Inca ruins, enhancing the overall experience.
