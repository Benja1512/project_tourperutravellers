Welcome to Cusco, the historical capital of Peru and a gateway to the magnificent Inca civilization. Nestled in the Andes at an altitude of 3,399 meters (11,152 feet), Cusco offers a unique blend of ancient history and vibrant culture, making it a must-visit destination for travelers. This UNESCO World Heritage Site boasts stunning colonial architecture built on the foundations of Inca temples, bustling markets, and a rich tapestry of traditions that reflect the city’s diverse heritage.

As the starting point for the iconic Inca Trail to Machu Picchu, Cusco draws adventurers and history enthusiasts alike, eager to explore its archaeological wonders, such as Sacsayhuamán, Qorikancha, and the Sacred Valley. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture by sampling delicious Peruvian cuisine, participating in colorful festivals, and engaging with the friendly locals. Whether you’re seeking adventure, history, or cultural experiences, Cusco promises an unforgettable journey through one of the most captivating regions of the world.

All the activities you can do on your trip to the Ayacucho City!

    Visit Qorikancha (Temple of the Sun) – Once the most important temple in the Inca Empire, Qorikancha was dedicated to the Sun God, Inti. Explore the impressive architecture and learn about its significance in Inca religion and history.
    Discover Sacsayhuamán – A short distance from the city center, this archaeological site features massive stone walls built by the Incas. Enjoy stunning views of Cusco and explore the surrounding area, which is great for hiking.
    Hike the Inca Trail – Embark on the Inca Trail trek to Machu Picchu, which offers breathtaking views of the Andes and visits to various archaeological sites along the way. Book in advance, as spots fill quickly.

Machu Picchu is one of the most iconic archaeological sites in the world and a symbol of the Inca Empire. Located in the Andes Mountains of Peru, approximately 2,430 meters (7,970 feet) above sea level, it offers breathtaking views and a deep connection to Inca history and culture. Here’s an overview of what makes Machu Picchu so special:

Architectural Marvel Inca Engineering: The site showcases the Inca's advanced engineering skills, including sophisticated agricultural terraces, intricate stonework, and a complex system of waterways. Construction: Machu Picchu is made of precisely cut stones that fit together without the use of mortar, demonstrating the Incas' mastery of stone masonry.
