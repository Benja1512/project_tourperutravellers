Explore Trujillo Through Videos

Trujillo is located on the northern coast of Peru, approximately 550 kilometers (about 342 miles) northwest of Lima, the capital city. Nestled along the banks of the Moche River, it is known for its rich history and vibrant culture. The city serves as a gateway to several important archaeological sites, including the ancient Moche and Chimu civilizations. Notable nearby attractions include the iconic Huacas del Sol y de la Luna, which showcase the impressive artistry and architectural prowess of the Moche culture, and the ruins of Chan Chan, the largest adobe city in the world. With its beautiful colonial architecture, bustling plazas, and close proximity to the Pacific Ocean, Trujillo is a fascinating destination for travelers interested in history and culture.

On your journey through Peru, the Colonial Archaeological Trujillo tour offers an enriching experience that combines ancient history, cultural exploration, and breathtaking coastal landscapes:

  • Huacas del Sol y de la Luna – Explore the impressive Moche archaeological sites, where you can learn about the ancient civilization’s religious practices and admire intricate murals and architecture.
  • Chan Chan – Visit the largest adobe city in the world, once the capital of the Chimu civilization, and marvel at its vast ruins and unique architectural designs.
  • Huanchaco Beach – Relax at this charming coastal town, famous for its traditional fishing boats called "caballitos de totora," and enjoy fresh seafood while taking in stunning ocean views.
